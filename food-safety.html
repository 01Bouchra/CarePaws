Food safety

How to feed your pet ?

Feeding pets is more than filling a bowl—it’s about safety and balance. Some human foods are toxic, so knowing what’s safe helps avoid health problems. The right diet keeps your pet happy, healthy, and thriving We’ve made a simple list of safe foods and ones to avoid, so you’ll always know what to feed your pet

Here’s a guide to foods that are safe for cats, as well as foods that should always be avoided. Following these guidelines is necessary!

Safe food for cats

Proteins:

Eggs, Liver, Lamb, Turkey, Chicken, Fish

Vegetables:

Peas, Broccoli, Asparagus, Spinach, Zucchini, Pumpkin, Sweet potatoes, Cucumber

Fruits:

Blueberries, Bananas, Honeydew melon, Cantaloupe, Watermelon, Apples

Grains & Others:

Oats, Polenta, Bread, Whole grains, Cheese (small portions)

Unsafe food for cats

Toxic Foods:

onions , Garlic , Chives , Chocolate , Grapes , Raisins , Alcohol , Avocado

Dairy & Fatty Foods

Milk , Cream , Cheese (large portions) , Coconut milk

Meats & Fish to Avoid :

Raw fish , Tuna , Bones

Other Dangerous Items :

Candy , Gum , Yeast/Dough , Nuts , Seeds , Too many treats , Dog food

Below is a guide to foods that are safe for dogs, along with foods that can be harmful or toxic. Using this information will help keep your dog safe, healthy, and full of energy.

Safe food for dogs

Proteins:

Meat (cooked, bones removed), Chicken, Turkey, Pork, Lean beef, Fish — salmon and sardines, Eggs (cooked), Yogurts

Fruits and Vegetables:

Bananas, Blueberries, Apples, Seedless watermelon, Tomatoes (small portions), Pumpkin, Carrots, Sweet potatoes, Broccoli

Grains:

Corn, Quinoa, Oatmeal, Rice, Bread (small amount of plain bread), Coconut, Honey, Popcorn

Unsafe food for dogs

Toxic Foods:

Chocolate, Grapes, Raisins, Onions, Garlic, Chives, Avocado, Alcohol

Dairy & Fatty Foods:

Milk, Cream, Cheese (large portions), Coconut milk

Meats & Fish to Avoid:

Raw fish, Tuna, Bones

Sugary & Processed Items:

Candy, Gum, Yeast/Dough, Nuts, Seeds, Too many treats

Other Foods to Avoid:

Dog food (for cats, if cross-feeding)

This guide is meant for those moments when you’re not sure if a human snack or leftover is safe for your pet. By checking the list before sharing, you can help keep your cat or dog healthy, happy, and safe because even small mistakes can make a big difference!
